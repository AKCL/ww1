• Discharge certificate

    When the armistice was signed and fighting ended on 11 November 1918, there were 56,684 New Zealand soldiers serving overseas. They were gradually brought back to New Zealand, the last troops arriving in 1920.

    Charles Hartley's certificate of discharge shows that he served abroad for 4 years and 83 days and was not discharged till 5 August 1919.
    Charles Hartley. Discharge certificate. 1919. From: Charles Hartley. Pay books and certificate of discharge. NZMS 1107.

  • Bonfire on Mount Eden

    Official peace celebrations were not held till July 1919 after the signing of the Treaty of Versailles.

    Of the 18,166 New Zealand dead, 12,483 died in France and Belgium, 2721 during the Gallipoli Campaign and 381 in Sinai and Palestine.

    Those who returned to New Zealand had to settle back into civilian life. Many who were maimed or suffering from shell-shock found this adjustment difficult or impossible.
    James Richardson. Bonfire on Mount Eden for the peace celebrations at the end of the First World War. 19 July 1919. Photo ref: 4-1515c.
