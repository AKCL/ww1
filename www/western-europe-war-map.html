• Map of the Western Front
    At the outbreak of war the German army quickly occupied most of Belgium and advanced on Paris. After the Battle of Marne in September 1914, the Germans were pushed back to the line that became the stalemate of the Western Front.

    The red lines on the map mark the limits of the movements of the armies throughout the duration of the war. The dotted line is the furthest German advance. The unbroken line is the front in November 1918 at the armistice. The concentration of lines in the north, near the English Channel, centres on the Belgian town of Ypres (Ieper). The village of Passchendaele is nearby. The Somme battlefield is south of Arras.
    The Daily Telegraph war map of Western Europe (No. 4). London: Geographia, about 1919. NZ map 7811.
